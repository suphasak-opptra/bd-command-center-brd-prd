Part 1

Business Requirements Document (BRD)

What the business needs and why

1.1   Executive Summary

Problem Statement

Opptra's BD team of 3 manages 100+ active leads across disconnected systems (Notion, Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets). There are no standardized stage definitions, no SLA tracking, no formal ownership model, and no automation. Verbal commitments are lost, rejected deals disappear, stakeholders are misaligned, and the team spends disproportionate time on admin rather than deal-making. The system is at breaking point.

Proposed Solution

Build the Opptra BD Command Center — a Notion-native CRM orchestrated by Claude AI with Gmail and Slack integrations. The system standardizes the deal pipeline (7 stages + Rejected/On Hold), enforces SLAs, assigns clear ownership via RACI, automates lead capture/notifications/escalations, and provides real-time pipeline visibility to all stakeholders.

Expected Outcomes

Near-zero lead leakage, 90%+ reduction in manual data entry, real-time stage visibility, automated stakeholder notifications, AI-powered revenue forecasting and red flag detection, and a scalable foundation that grows with the team.

Part 2

Product Requirements Document (PRD)

How to build it — specifications, schemas, and workflows

2.1   Product Overview & Personas

Product: Opptra BD Command Center
Vision: A single system where every brand deal is visible, every action is tracked, every SLA is enforced, and AI handles the operations work — so the BD team can focus on relationships and closing.

User Personas

BD Manager (Primary)

Goal: Manage 30-40 leads efficiently without dropping any

Pain: Spends 1-2 hrs/day on data entry and status updates instead of selling

Needs: Automated lead capture, SLA reminders, one-click stage updates, deal history at a glance

Brand Team Lead

Goal: Receive clean, complete deal handoffs at Signed stage

Pain: Gets surprised by deals they weren't tracking; missing context at handoff

Needs: Visibility into SQL+ deals, complete deal package at handoff, no verbal surprises

Central/Support Team

Goal: Respond to BD requests with clear context and reasonable timelines

Pain: Gets ad-hoc requests via Slack with no context; unclear priority

Needs: Structured support requests linked to deals, clear deadlines

CEO / Leadership

Goal: Understand pipeline health and make investment/approval decisions

Pain: No reliable pipeline data; learns about deals too late

Needs: Weekly pipeline digest, revenue forecast, escalation on big deals

2.2   User Stories

BD Manager Stories

US-01: As a BD Manager, I want new brand inquiries from Gmail to auto-create deals in Notion so that no lead goes untracked.
US-02: As a BD Manager, I want to see how many days each deal has been in its current stage so I can prioritize stale deals.
US-03: As a BD Manager, I want SLA reminders sent to me via Slack before a deal breaches its time limit so I can take action.
US-04: As a BD Manager, I want to advance a deal to the next stage with one click and have the system auto-notify relevant stakeholders.
US-05: As a BD Manager, I want verbal commitments I make in Slack to be captured on the deal record so the Brand Team isn't blindsided.
US-06: As a BD Manager, I want to reject a deal with a reason code so the pipeline reflects reality and the deal can be revived later.
US-07: As a BD Manager, I want AI to flag deals with red flags (distributor conflicts, bad terms) so I don't waste time on unviable opportunities.

Brand Team Stories

US-08: As a Brand Team Lead, I want to see all deals at SQL stage and above so I can prepare for upcoming handoffs.
US-09: As a Brand Team Lead, I want the deal record to contain all analyses, decks, and communications when ownership transfers to me so I don't have to re-do work.
US-10: As a Brand Team Lead, I want to be automatically notified when a deal reaches Signed with a complete deal package.

Central/Support Team Stories

US-11: As a Central Team member, I want to receive support requests as tagged tasks linked to specific deals so I have full context.
US-12: As a Central Team member, I want to see my pending support requests in one view so I can prioritize my work.

Leadership Stories

US-13: As CEO, I want a weekly pipeline digest showing deal flow, revenue forecast, SLA compliance, and top deals requiring my attention.
US-14: As CEO, I want to be alerted when a high-value deal (>$500K) reaches Opportunity stage so I can provide input.
US-15: As CEO, I want to see the reason distribution for rejected deals to understand why we're losing opportunities.

System/AI Stories

US-16: As the system, I want to prevent duplicate deal creation by matching brand names and contacts against existing records.
US-17: As the system, I want to auto-calculate revenue potential when a deal reaches MQL based on brand data and market comparables.
US-18: As the system, I want to auto-remind deal owners of On-Hold deals when their revival date arrives.

WF-06: Revenue Potential Update P1

Trigger: Deal reaches MQL OR brand data is updated

Steps:1. Claude analyzes brand category, market size, channel presence, comparable brands in portfolio → 2. Estimates revenue range (low/mid/high) → 3. Assigns confidence score (Low/Medium/High) → 4. Updates Revenue Potential and Revenue Confidence fields → 5. Calculates Weighted Revenue based on stage probability → 6. Logs estimate in Activity Log for audit
Note:Revenue estimates are always ranges, never single numbers. Human override is always allowed and logged.

WF-07: Red Flag Detection & Alert P1

Trigger: Deal created or deal details updated

Steps:1. Claude scans deal and brand data against rejection criteria → 2. If match found: add Red Flag tag(s) to deal → 3. If critical flag: block advancement past MQL + alert BD Lead → 4. If non-critical: add warning but allow advancement → 5. Log detection in Activity Log
Criteria:Distributor conflict (critical), Low margin (critical), Financial instability (warning), Incompatible business model (critical), Regulatory risk (warning)
WF-08: Weekly Pipeline Report Generation  P1

Trigger: Scheduled — every Monday 8:00 AM

Steps: Claude queries all active deals → calculates stage distribution, SLA compliance %, revenue forecast, top 5 deals by value, deals needing attention → generates formatted digest → sends via Slack #bd-pipeline and email to leadership

WF-09: On-Hold Deal Revival Check  P1

Trigger: Daily check — compare today's date to Revival Date on all On-Hold deals

Steps: If Revival Date = today → Slack DM to original deal owner: "⏰ [Deal Name] revival date is today. Please review and re-engage or extend hold." → If no action in 7 days → escalate to BD Lead

WF-10: Work Deduplication Check  P1

Trigger: New deal creation OR document upload

Steps: Claude checks Brands DB for existing analyses, scouting reports, decks for the same brand → if found, links existing documents to new deal and notifies owner: "📎 Existing materials found for [Brand] — linked to your deal."

2.6   Claude AI Automation Specifications

AUTO-001   Gmail Lead Detection P0

TriggerNew email in BD inbox from brand-related sender or containing brand inquiry keywords
InputEmail subject, body, sender name/domain, thread history
ProcessingClaude classifies intent (inquiry / follow-up / irrelevant), extracts brand name, contact info, opportunity details, urgency signals
Output (>80% conf.)New Deal record in Notion (Connect stage) + email thread linked + Slack notification to BD team
Output (<80% conf.)Flag for manual review → Slack DM to BD lead: "📧 Possible new lead from [sender] — needs review"
Error handlingIf Notion API fails, retry 3x then alert BD lead. If email parsing fails, log error and flag for manual review.

AUTO-002   Slack Commitment Capture P0

TriggerSlack message in BD channels containing commitment language (e.g., "we'll send them…", "agreed to…", "committed to…", "promised…")
InputMessage text, channel, sender, thread context, mentioned deal/brand names
ProcessingClaude identifies: commitment/action item, deadline (explicit or implied), responsible party, related deal
OutputUpdate deal's Notes field with commitment text + set Next Action and Next Action Due + DM deal owner confirmation
Confidence gateIf deal match is ambiguous, ask in thread: "Did you mean [Deal A] or [Deal B]?"

AUTO-003   SLA Monitor P0

TriggerDaily scheduled run (6:00 AM) + real-time on stage entry
InputAll active deals: Stage, Stage Entry Date, SLA Limit
ProcessingCalculate Days in Stage / SLA Limit ratio for each deal
Thresholds75%: Warning DM → 100%: Alert DM + channel → 125%: Auto-action (per stage rules)
De-duplicationOnly send each alert level once per deal (don't re-alert daily for same threshold)

AUTO-004   Revenue Potential Calculator P1

TriggerDeal reaches MQL stage OR brand data updated in Brands DB
InputBrand category, market size data, channel presence, comparable brands in Opptra portfolio, deal terms (if available)
ProcessingClaude estimates annual revenue potential using: category benchmarks, market penetration rates, comparable brand performance
OutputRevenue Potential (range), Revenue Confidence (Low/Med/High), calculation rationale in Activity Log
OverrideHuman can always override with manual estimate — logged as "Manual Override" in Activity Log

AUTO-005   Red Flag Detector P1

TriggerDeal created OR deal/brand details updated
InputBrand financials, current distributors, proposed terms, business model, regulatory status
ProcessingClaude screens against configurable rejection criteria (see Business Rules BR-108)
Output (critical)Red Flag tag added + advancement blocked past MQL + Slack alert to BD Lead
Output (warning)Red Flag tag added + warning note on deal + no block

AUTO-006   Stakeholder Update Broadcaster P0

TriggerStage transition, significant deal update, SLA breach, deal signed
InputDeal details, event type, RACI matrix, stakeholder contact info
ProcessingClaude generates contextual summary of the event, identifies who needs to know per RACI
OutputSlack notification to appropriate channel/DM + email if configured for that stakeholder
TemplatesStage advance: "🎯 [Deal] moved to [Stage] by [Actor]. Next: [Action]." | SLA: "⚠️ [Deal] SLA warning..." | Signed: "🎉 [Deal] is SIGNED!"

AUTO-007   Duplicate Detection P1

TriggerNew deal creation (manual or auto)
InputBrand name, contact email/name
ProcessingFuzzy match against existing Deals and Brands databases (Levenshtein distance ≤ 2 for names, exact match for emails)
Output (match)Warning: "⚠️ Possible duplicate — [Existing Deal] matches. [Link]" → block auto-create, flag for human decision
Output (no match)Proceed with creation
